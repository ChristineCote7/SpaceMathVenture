MathVenture

This quiz contains the following topics: Definite Integrals, Derivatives as a Rate of Change, Problems about Growth, Continuity of a Function, Limit Theorems. Goodluck!

1. The population of Planet Mercury is currently 10,000 and is growing at a rate proportional to its current population. The growth rate is estimated to be 2% per year. Determine the population of Planet Mercury after 10 years.

2. Astronomers have been observing a comet named Harley as it travels through space. They have determined that the distance (in kilometers) of the comet from Earth at any given time can be written by the function: D(t) = 1000t^2 / (t^2 + 1). t represents the time in years since the observation began. Find the limiting distance of the comet as time approaches infinity.

3. A rocket ship is blasting off into space, and the height of the rocket above the ground (in meters) at time t seconds is given by the function: h(t) = 5t + 10, for 0 ≤ t ≤ 10. t represents the time passed since the rocket took off. Determine if the height function h(t) is continuous at t = 5.

4. A satellite orbits the planet Mars in a circular path. The distance between the satellite and the planet at time t is given by the function D(t) = 24t^2 + 4t + 5. Time is in seconds and D(t) represents the distance in meters. Find the velocity of the satellite at t = 8 seconds and interpret it as a rate of change.

5. A cylindrical fuel tank is filled with rocket fuel. The tank has a radius of 5 meters. The spacecraft uses the fuel during takeoff and it decreases at a rate of 35 m^3/s. How fast does the height of the fuel drop?

6. You are a scientist working on a space mission that involves a rocket. The velocity v(t) of your spacecraft, measured in meters per second, at time t seconds after launch, is described by the function: v(t)=6^2−9t+14, find the acceleration of the rocket if the time is 12 seconds.

7. A space probe is traveling along a straight path. The probe’s velocity at a time is given by the function v(t)=4t^2+7t+6, where v(t) is measured in m/s. Determine the probe’s acceleration at t=4 secs.

8. The population of aliens on a planet is growing exponentially at a continuous rate of 3% per year. At the beginning of the year 2020, the population was 30,000. Determine the population at the beginning of the year 2030.

9. A rocket takes off from the ground: While it takes off, a meteor flies through space, The distance (s) in meters that the rocket travels from the ground after (t) seconds is given by s(t) = -20t^2 + 660t while the distance (s) in meters that the meteor travels from space is given by s(t) = -35t^2 + 660t. Is the rocket fast enough to avoid the meteor with the velocity of the given time of 5 seconds after its take-off?

10. A meteor is heading to space x rocketship. The speed of the meteor in meters per second is v(t) 9 * 8 + Vo where t is the number of seconds since the location of the meteor was detected and Vois the initial speed of the meteor (meters per second). If the meteor travels _ meters during the first _ seconds after its location was detected, what is the initial speed of the meteor?
